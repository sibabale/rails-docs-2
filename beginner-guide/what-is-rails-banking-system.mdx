# What is the Rails Banking System?

Welcome! If you're new to banking technology, software development, or APIs, this guide will help you understand what Rails is and why it matters in simple, clear terms.

## Think of Rails Like Digital Banking Plumbing

Imagine your house has pipes that carry water from the city supply to your faucets. Banks work similarly - they need "digital pipes" to move money safely between accounts, businesses, and other banks.

**Rails is that digital plumbing system for banks and financial companies.**

**For Complete Beginners**: Don't worry if terms like "API" or "microservices" are new to you. We'll explain everything step by step!
</Note>

## Why Do Banks Need Systems Like Rails?

### The Old Way (Problems)
Before modern banking systems:
- Banks used paper ledgers (like giant notebooks)
- Transferring money took days or weeks
- International transfers were extremely slow and expensive
- It was hard to track where money went
- Human errors were common and costly

### The New Way (Rails Solution)
With Rails banking infrastructure:
- Everything is digital and instant
- Money moves in seconds, not days
- All transactions are automatically recorded
- Multiple safety checks prevent errors
- Works 24/7 without human intervention

  <Step title="Rails checks everything is safe">

## Common Banking Terms Explained

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="API (Application Programming Interface)">
    **Simple Explanation**: A way for different computer programs to talk to each other
    
    **Banking Example**: When you check your balance in a mobile app, the app uses an API to ask the bank's computers for your account information
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Microservices">
    **Simple Explanation**: Breaking a large system into smaller, specialized pieces
    
    **Banking Example**: Instead of one giant program handling everything, Rails has separate services for users, accounts, transactions, etc. If one piece needs updating, the others keep working
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="SDK (Software Development Kit)">
    **Simple Explanation**: A toolkit that makes it easier for programmers to build applications
    
    **Banking Example**: Instead of writing complex code from scratch, developers can use Rails SDK to quickly add banking features to their apps
  </AccordionItem>
  
  <AccordionItem title="Database">
    **Simple Explanation**: A digital filing cabinet where all information is stored
    
    **Banking Example**: Customer information, account balances, and transaction records are all stored in secure databases
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>
